# Competitor Audit — Return on Reno vs. the Top Similar Tools

**Date:** 26 June 2026. **Scope:** the highest-ranking sites for "renovation cost calculator" and "renovation / cost-vs-value ROI calculator," assessed on tools, navigation, and look & feel, then compared to returnonreno.

## The 5 we benchmarked (and why)
| Site | What it is | Closeness to us |
|---|---|---|
| **sum.money** (/home-renovation-roi-calculator) | A renovation **ROI** calculator on Cost-vs-Value data, multi-country | ★★★★★ near-identical positioning — our most direct competitor |
| **Remodelum** | All-in-one renovation **management** app: estimate → quote-compare → expense-track | ★★★ same topic, different model (SaaS) |
| **Homewyse** | The long-tail **cost-estimate** authority — thousands of "cost to install X" pages | ★★★ same topic, cost-only, huge scale |
| **Fixr** | **Editorial + data**: cost guides, "Cost vs Value [year]" with charts, ROI-by-state | ★★★ content/authority play |
| **NerdWallet / SoFi** | Finance-brand renovation **cost calculators** inside a big tool suite | ★★ high authority, generic tool |

## Site-by-site

### 1. sum.money — the direct threat
**Tools:** three modes in one calculator — **Project ROI**, **Compare Projects** (enter a budget, split it across 2–3 projects, see blended ROI), and **Regional ROI** (by US Census region). Plus a **custom project-cost input** (paste your contractor quote), **home-value %**, a **Share** button that encodes the whole scenario in the URL, a worked **example**, an **embeddable iframe** widget, and a sidebar of **6 related finance calculators** (affordability, HELOC, home-sale tax, property tax, closing cost, rental). Multi-country (12+ locales). No cookies, no sign-up.
**Navigation:** flat, fast, calculator-first; related-calculator cross-links.
**Look & feel:** clean and functional but plain/utilitarian (dark accent, system-ish type, little warmth or motion).
**What they have that we don't:** compare/budget-split mode · custom-quote input · shareable URLs · embeddable widget · a finance-calculator suite.

### 2. Remodelum — renovation management SaaS
**Tools:** ZIP-code **cost estimator** with **itemized labor + materials** line items; per-room estimators (kitchen, bath, addition, basement, deck); **contractor-quote comparison**; **expense/budget tracker**; **capital-improvements log** (tax cost-basis). Free estimate, sign-up for the app.
**Navigation:** deep mega-menu (Calculators / Planning / Management / Solutions / Resources) — signals depth, slightly heavy.
**Look & feel:** modern SaaS, screenshots, segmented by audience (homeowners / contractors / investors).
**What they have that we don't:** ZIP-level precision · itemized labor/material breakdown · quote comparison · expense tracker · capital-improvements log.

### 3. Homewyse — the cost-estimate giant
**Tools:** enormous library of per-task calculators — "cost to install/replace/repair X" — each with labor hours, material costs and fair-price ranges, ZIP-adjusted. Owns the long-tail "cost to [task]" queries.
**Navigation:** utilitarian top menu (Materials / Installation / Maintenance / Reference).
**Look & feel:** **dated** — dense tables, heavy ads, little design. We clearly beat them here.
**What they have that we don't:** breadth of granular per-task cost pages · explicit labor-hours data.

### 4. Fixr — editorial + data authority
**Tools:** cost guides + the annual **"Cost vs Value"** piece with **data visualizations** (sunburst + YoY charts), ROI-by-state articles, a siding calculator, named **expert authors** with bylines (E-E-A-T).
**Navigation:** article/category driven.
**Look & feel:** editorial, chart-led, credible — but ad-heavy.
**What they have that we don't:** published **annual data study** as a link magnet · **named expert authorship** · rich infographics.

### 5. NerdWallet / SoFi — finance brands
**Tools:** a renovation cost calculator embedded in a huge suite of money tools + explainer content.
**Look & feel:** polished but generic; the calculator isn't the star.
**What they have that we don't:** domain authority · a broad calculator ecosystem · financing cross-sell.

## How we compare

### Where Return on Reno already wins
- **Look & feel (clear lead).** After the 3-phase redesign — warm palette, Fraunces/Inter type, animated result with a recoup meter, colour-coded ROI bars, icon project picker, hero illustration, sticky mobile bar — we are the **best-designed** tool in this set. Homewyse is dated, Fixr/NerdWallet are ad-heavy, Remodelum is corporate-SaaS, sum.money is clean-but-plain.
- **Honesty & clarity.** Our "net cost is not a loss," the >100% explainer, the pool climate note — clearer and more trustworthy framing than most.
- **Programmatic depth.** 560 project×region pages is a strong, modern SEO footprint (Homewyse-scale ambition, far better execution).
- **Global + no friction.** Multi-currency, no sign-up, no cookies — on par with sum.money, ahead of the SaaS/finance players.

### Where we're behind (tools we lack)
1. **Compare / budget-split mode** — sum.money's best feature. Pick a budget, split across projects, see blended ROI. We have the data; this is the biggest functional gap.
2. **Custom-cost / "enter your quote" input** — override the national average with your own number. Trivial to add, high utility.
3. **Shareable result URLs + "copy result"** — virality and backlinks. We have none.
4. **Embeddable widget (iframe)** — sum.money offers this; it's a backlink engine. Already on our roadmap.
5. **Annual "Renovation ROI Index" data study** with charts — Fixr's authority/link magnet. On our roadmap.
6. **Itemized labor/material split** — Remodelum & Homewyse show it; adds credibility to each cost figure.
7. **Named expert author / reviewer (E-E-A-T)** — Fixr and NerdWallet have credentialed bylines; we're an anonymous "team."
8. **Depth of nav / a Tools + Guides hub** — competitors signal depth with resource menus; we have one guide and a shallow nav.
9. **(Strategic) adjacent finance calculators** — sum.money/NerdWallet cross-link affordability, HELOC, home-sale-tax, etc., deepening sessions and pages.

### Navigation read
Ours is **clean and easy** (Calculator · Project costs · Best ROI · About) — better than Homewyse's clutter, simpler than Remodelum's mega-menu. The flip side: it's **shallow**. As we add tools/guides we should introduce a light **Tools** and **Guides** grouping so the breadth is discoverable without becoming cluttered.

---

## Recommendations (prioritised)

**Tier 1 — close the calculator gap (highest impact, uses data we already have)**
1. **Add a "Compare / split my budget" mode** to the calculator (match sum.money). Enter a budget + pick 2–3 projects → blended ROI and value-added. This is our biggest missing tool and plays to our strength.
2. **Add a custom-cost input** ("Got a quote? Enter it") to override the national average.
3. **Shareable result URLs** — encode calculator state in the query string + a "copy link" button. Cheap virality and backlinks.

**Tier 2 — distribution & authority**
4. **Embeddable calculator widget** (iframe) — turn other sites into backlink sources.
5. **Publish an annual "Renovation ROI Index"** page with a couple of charts (our league data) — a citable link magnet, à la Fixr.
6. **Add 3–4 high-intent guides** (kitchen vs bathroom ROI, does a pool add value, renovate-to-sell checklist) and introduce **Guides + Tools** nav groupings.

**Tier 3 — credibility & breadth**
7. **Named author / expert reviewer** byline + a richer methodology/sources section (E-E-A-T parity with Fixr/NerdWallet).
8. **Optional: a simple labor vs materials split** on cost figures (credibility vs Remodelum/Homewyse).
9. **Optional/strategic: 2–3 adjacent calculators** (e.g., home-equity/HELOC-for-renovation, "how much to spend vs home value") to broaden the suite and internal linking.

### One-line verdict
We already **out-design** the field; the gap is **tool breadth on the calculator itself** (compare-mode, custom cost, sharing) and **distribution/authority** (embed widget, data study, expert byline). Closing Tier 1 would make our core tool match or beat sum.money while keeping our visual edge.
